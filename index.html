Here are some of the industry’s best practices that other pool contractors wish you did not know.

Proper pump installation will keep the motor dry and away from splashing or spraying. Also, it must be installed in an area that will give it proper circulation around the motor. Inspect it regularly and make sure that dirt and debris haven’t collected near it. If you have a cover on the motor, make sure it’s not collecting condensation.

Changing Pool Filters

Filters need to be changed at a minimum of every year or two. However, there are times when it might need to be changed sooner. Inspect your filter monthly for frayed fabric or a collapsed core in the cartridge. Check the pressure gauge every week and make sure it is within the normal range of 10-25 PSI. A clogged filter can cause the pressure to be too high.

Checking for leaks in the pool is another important part of maintenance. You can have leaks in the pump, filter, heater, pipes, or the chlorinator. To see if your pool is leaking, you can use the bucket test. Put a 5-gallon bucket on the second step of your pool. Add water to the bucket to match the current water level. Then, use a permanent marker to mark the level inside of the bucket. Turn off the pump and wait for 24 hours. If the water in your bucket and the pool has gone down, but are even, this is normal evaporation. However, if the bucket is higher than the pool, you have a leak.
